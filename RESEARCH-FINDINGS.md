## 1977-03-18 — Winterland Arena, San Francisco, CA
**THE FIRST SCARLET > FIRE**

### Primary Source Findings

**Musical History:**
- "Fire on the Mountain" had its LIVE DEBUT on this night, emerging out of Scarlet Begonias for the first time ever. The song had never been played live before.
- The debut pairing featured "uncertain, hasty transition into Fire and some lyrical imprecision; clearly they were relieved to find their way" but was "very pretty nonetheless."
- SOURCE: [Dead Essays - Scarlet>Fire Guest Post](https://deadessays.blogspot.com/2017/03/scarletfire-guest-post.html)

**Jerry Garcia Quote (1988):**
- Garcia later reflected on the Scarlet>Fire transition: "the transition itself would be a piece of music." He also noted that after hundreds of performances, "we've worn the pathways."
- SOURCE: [Rock and Roll Nerd](https://rockandrollnerd.wordpress.com/2013/03/18/35-years-ago-today-the-first-ever-scarlet-fire/)

**Context:**
- Keith Olsen was producing Terrapin Station at this time, forcing the band to "rehearse endlessly, which had a major impact on their live performances during the spring of 1977." (Library of Congress essay, Scott W. Allen)
- Garcia was playing his Travis Bean TB 500 guitar, which he'd been using since summer 1976.
- After March 18, 1977, Scarlet and Fire appeared together in 254 Dead shows.
- SOURCE: [Extra Chill - Timeline of Scarlet>Fire](https://extrachill.com/the-timeline-of-scarlet-begonias-fire-on-the-mountain/); [Mickey Hart official site](https://mickeyhart.net/news/fire-on-the-mountain-fridays-debut-performance-march-18th-1977-at-winterland-arena-5491/)

**Venue:**
- Winterland Arena, corner of Post and Steiner Streets, San Francisco. Opened 1928 as New Dreamland Auditorium (skating rink), renamed Winterland late 1930s. Capacity 5,400. Bill Graham began regular weekend shows after closing Fillmore West in 1971. The Dead played Winterland 73 times between 1967 and 1978. Now demolished — condos occupy the site.

**Photography:**
- No professional documentation found for March 1977 run specifically.

**Newspaper Reviews:**
- No San Francisco Chronicle or Examiner reviews located for this date.

## 1977-04-30 — The Palladium, New York, NY

### Primary Source Findings

**Venue History:**
- The Palladium (formerly Academy of Music), 126 E. 14th Street, NYC. Originally built 1927 by architect Thomas W. Lamb for film mogul William Fox as a 3,000-seat movie palace. Renamed "The Palladium" in September 1976.
- After Fillmore East closed in 1971, it became NYC's premier mid-range rock venue. Operated with dual identity: concerts 3-4 evenings weekly (8 and 11:30 PM shows) and kung-fu movies during daytime.
- Featured a large crystal chandelier that audiences feared would crash and a sound system designed by Richard Long & Associates.
- Demolished in 1997 by NYU to build a dormitory (also named Palladium, opened 2001).
- SOURCE: [Jerry's Brokendown Palaces](http://jerrygarciasbrokendownpalaces.blogspot.com/2011/09/academy-of-music-126-e-14th-street-new.html)

**The Run:**
- The Dead played six nights: April 29-30, May 1, 3-4, 1977. The opening night ran nearly five and a half hours.

**John Rockwell (New York Times) Review:**
- A John Rockwell NYT review of the Palladium shows is confirmed to exist but the full text was not accessible through online sources. Referenced in multiple fan sources.

**Dave's Picks Volume 50:**
- The May 3-4 shows were released as Dave's Picks Volume 50. The reviewer notes the Dead applied "rigorous discipline" from their Terrapin Station sessions with Keith Olsen, demonstrating "practiced authority" and "collective synchrony perhaps unequaled in their history." Phil Lesh paradoxically observed that "something was missing."
- SOURCE: [Glide Magazine review](https://glidemagazine.com/301217/grateful-dead-daves-picks-volume-50-the-palladium-new-york-ny-5-3-77-album-review/)

## 1977-05-18 — Fox Theatre, Atlanta, GA

### Primary Source Findings

**Venue History:**
- Fox Theatre opened Christmas Day 1929. Originally conceived as a grand headquarters for Atlanta's Shriners. Design inspired by Spain's Alhambra and Egypt's Temple of Karnak. Features "soaring domes, minarets, intricate gold leaf, and stunning trompe l'oeil artwork."
- Built at cost of $2,228,670.50. Capacity approximately 4,665 seats. Stage: 135 feet wide but only 38 feet deep.
- Houses "Mighty Mo" organ: 3,622 pipes, second-largest theater organ in the country (behind Radio City Music Hall).
- Faced demolition in 1974. Community activists launched "Save The Fox" campaign, raising $3 million through community donations. Reopened 1975.
- SOURCE: [Fox Theatre official site](https://www.foxtheatre.org/about-us/fox-history); [Georgia Encyclopedia](https://www.georgiaencyclopedia.org/articles/arts-culture/fox-theatre/)

**Performance:**
- Emphasized Garcia ballad work: reggae-influenced Jack-A-Roe, near-perfect Friend of the Devil, It Must've Been the Roses, High Time.
- Second set: Estimated Prophet > Eyes of the World > Drums > The Other One > Stella Blue > Around and Around. The Stella Blue ran 15+ minutes, described as "exquisitely slow and luscious."
- SOURCE: [Liner Notes Music Blog](https://linernotesmusicblog.wordpress.com/2016/05/20/grateful-dead-weekly-13-fox-theater-atlanta-ga-518-1977/)

## 1977-09-03 — Raceway Park, Englishtown, NJ

### Primary Source Findings

**Original Newspaper Review:**
- **Martha Megill**, Asbury Park Press, September 6, 1977. Titles: "Concert Attendance Takes Planning" (fine print) / "Grateful Dead Fan Is Happy, Satisfied" (bold).
- Megill describes her three-mile walk from parking to the concert area.
- Megill: "Truckin', the last song before the encore, was the highlight of the entire day" and "One hundred thousand people [in attendance] were ecstatic."
- She ends: "I agree wholeheartedly with the many bumper stickers I saw. 'There Ain't Nothin' Like a Grateful Dead Concert.'"
- This review was included in Dick's Picks Volume 15 liner notes.
- SOURCE: Referenced in [NJ1015](https://nj1015.com/the-time-the-grateful-dead-played-to-150000-people-in-englishtown/)

**Attendance & Scale:**
- Tickets sold: 102,000 (per promoter John Scher). Estimates range 100,000-150,000.
- Bill: New Riders of the Purple Sage + Marshall Tucker Band + Grateful Dead.
- Hot, humid September day. First Dead performance after a three-month summer hiatus.
- Mickey Hart's return gig following an automobile accident.
- First performance of "Truckin'" in over two years.

**Legal/Political Context:**
- Neighboring town of Manalapan sued to prevent the concert, citing fears of "motorcycle gangs, drugs, and violence." A judge refused to stop the show.
- **John Scher (promoter):** "This was the height of the generation gap. The mayor thought that Armageddon was coming. They sued to try to stop the show. We had 100,000 people there and it was fantastic."
- The lawsuit's publicity likely boosted attendance (per Scher).
- SOURCE: [NJArts.net](https://www.njarts.net/eyes-world-grateful-dead/)

**Medical/Incidents:**
- Cases of dehydration and fall injuries.
- Two deaths: one person choked to death; another died in a car crash leaving the venue.
- At least 50 people transported to Jersey Shore Medical Center.
- Despite incidents, authorities noted the massive crowd was "remarkably well behaved." No motorcycle gangs appeared.
- SOURCE: [NJ1015](https://nj1015.com/the-time-the-grateful-dead-played-to-150000-people-in-englishtown/)

**Tech Milestone:**
- First rock concert where a music writer covered the event using a "portable" computer (Teleram P-1800 terminal, ~60 pounds, transmitted via acoustic modem).
- SOURCE: [Jewish Community Voice](https://www.jewishvoicesnj.org/articles/recalling-a-tech-milestone-with-the-grateful-dead-40-years-ago/)

**Photography:**
- James R. Anderson photographed the show, including shots of the soundboard crew during Marshall Tucker Band's set and the massive crowd.
- SOURCE: [James R Anderson Photography](https://jranderson.photoshelter.com/image/I0000QfM91vVEVb0)

# ROBERT CHRISTGAU — "The Grateful Dead in Four Dimensions"
## Village Voice, June 13, 1977

### Full Analysis (available at robertchristgau.substack.com and robertchristgau.com)

**Key Quotes & Observations:**

- Reviews a concert film shot at Winterland in fall 1974 (the "farewell" shows).
- Technical innovation: "the sound mix follows the cut" — volume adjusts realistically as camera moves between musicians. "Redeems the genre" of live rock documentaries.
- The band was "obviously up for these shows," performing both "for the camera" and "for old times' sake."
- On Garcia: "the master of rock and roll improvisation—improvisation that doesn't distract from the beat." Close-ups captured "the hesitations and second thoughts that go into" his playing.
- On Keith Godchaux: "ranges effortlessly between Jerry Lee and Thelonious."
- On Donna Godchaux: "strength proves anything but ornamental."
- On Kreutzmann: appeared clumsy on film; energy improved when Mickey Hart joined for encore.
- Criticism: too much original material in the setlist was "a little flat" without their typical rock cover interpretations. "Morning Dew" deserved more attention for its "tone-poem meander."
- Personal history: followed the Dead "for nearly a decade," though "never a true Deadhead." Stopped attending around 1979 after a Madison Square Garden show "that never came together."
- Final verdict: "They were a great band."

SOURCE: [Christgau Substack](https://robertchristgau.substack.com/p/the-big-lookback-the-grateful-dead); [robertchristgau.com](https://www.robertchristgau.com/xg/dontstop/grateful-25.php)

# LIBRARY OF CONGRESS ESSAY — Cornell 5/8/77
## By Scott W. Allen (National Recording Preservation Board, 2011)

### Key Details (USABLE FOR CONTEXT ON ADJACENT SHOWS):

- **Ticket price:** $6.50 advance, $7.50 at door
- **Venue capacity:** 5,000 (Barton Hall), but as many as 6,000 crammed in
- **Weather:** Sunny 60-degree afternoon gave way to "blustery conditions, temperatures plummeting into the low 40s and sleet and rain." Half-foot of snow by show's end.
- **Doors chaos:** Only one door opened, creating dangerous crush. Ticketless fans snuck in. Security (Concert Commission students in red-and-white jerseys) overwhelmed.
- **Concert Commission:** Chaired by Robert Horowitz '78. Mike McEvoy booked the show: "It took a lot of arm-twisting over many months."
- **Keith Godchaux:** Seen "asleep on a folding table backstage" (per Brad Krakow '78).
- **Bill Kreutzmann:** Had a wrist injury — one of the few shows without a drum solo.
- **Garcia quote during show:** "All these people in front are getting horribly smashed. That means all you people in the back have to move back."
- **Phil Lesh after Mama Tried:** "Thanks, Mom" (it was Mother's Day).
- **Attendee Rick Bleier:** "I have a clear-as-yesterday memory of Garcia's face as he shook his head and grinned from ear-to-Cheshire-cat-ear."
- **Attendee Bleier on reputation:** "I believe the near-mythical reputation of this show originated from those who were there, rather than from those who heard the tapes."
- **Dick Latvala (Dead tape archivist)** on 5/22/77 Pembroke Pines: "May 22 tears apart Ithaca."
- **Blair Jackson (historian):** "May 8 is among the most collected, traded and downloaded concerts by any band ever. That's not hyperbole, either."
- **Charlie Rogers (tape collector):** "Add a cool concert poster [designed by graduating Cornell senior Jay Maybrey] and throw in a few salty tales..."
- **Jerry Moore:** Renowned taper whose "meticulous recording became the most-commonly traded version."
- **Post-show:** Pop's Diner, a block off campus, "filled to capacity with other hungry but contented Deadheads."
- **Barbara Lang '77 (hospitality committee):** Cooked meals for musicians and crew. "I loved cooking for the roadies, usually more than the performers."
- **Barton Hall:** Archaic athletic facility opened in 1915. Hosted three Dead shows total (1977, 1980, 1981).

## 1979-04-22 — Spartan Stadium, San Jose State University, San Jose, CA
**BRENT MYDLAND'S DEBUT**

### Primary Source Findings

**How Brent Joined:**

- "A much better onstage singer, technically, than Donna Godchaux had been, so immediately the group's harmonies took on a new power and vitality."
- "Garcia, who was still at the beginning of a long slide into addiction when Brent joined the group, was clearly buoyed by his presence."

**Performance:**
- Debuted with "Jack Straw." Made his presence felt on "Mexicali Blues" and "Sugaree."
- Second set Good Lovin' featured his "swirling organ work and vocals."
- "What's notable about the show is just how well Brent performs in his first concert. It doesn't sound that much different from where he'd be six months from now."
- Brent "helped foster a bit of change in the arrangements, with there being a lot more keyboards than ever before."

## 1979-10-27 — Cape Cod Coliseum, South Yarmouth, MA

### Primary Source Findings

**Venue History:**
- Cape Cod Coliseum, 46,000-square-foot concrete arena on White's Path in South Yarmouth. Opened September 1972. Built for $1.5 million by local real estate agent William Harrison.
- Capacity: 5,000-6,500 (up to 7,200 depending on configuration).
- Nicknamed the "Old Sweatbox" due to poor ventilation and heat during summer concerts. "The walls would rattle, there was no air conditioning. The sound was not pristine."
- Billboard ranked it New England's fifth-largest facility by 1974.
- The Grateful Dead played the coliseum only twice (October 27-28, 1979).
- In 1979, Vince McMahon bought the building. Final event June 4, 1984 (wrestling). Sold to Christmas Tree Shops, converted to warehouse. Building still stands, name faintly visible on facade.
- SOURCE: [Cape Cod LIFE](https://capecodlife.com/a-mecca-of-glaze-haze-the-cape-cod-coliseum/); [Cape & Islands NPR](https://www.capeandislands.org/in-this-place/2018-07-16/the-cape-cod-coliseum-brought-rock-royalty-to-south-yarmouth)

**Concert Experience:**
- Parking lot scene: "Everything went on in that parking lot" (attendee quote). Concerts resembled "mini Woodstock" events.
- SOURCE: [Cape & Islands NPR](https://www.capeandislands.org/in-this-place/2018-07-16/the-cape-cod-coliseum-brought-rock-royalty-to-south-yarmouth)

**Performance:**
- Included in the "30 Trips around the Sun" 80-disc box set as the representative 1979 show.
- Dancin' In The Streets opener: "tight jamming with music that swirls like a sea of city lights seen from above." 14 minutes.
- Franklin's Tower: "Major workout" — 17 minutes. "Towering trumpets that howl at the sky, punctuated by the intimacy of Jerry's voice."
- The Other One arrives "like a stalking beast" with Phil's bass creating overwhelming force.
- SOURCE: [Dead Listening Guide](https://www.deadlistening.com/2008/09/1979-october-27-cape-cod-coliseum.html)

## 1980-10-04 — Warfield Theatre, San Francisco, CA

### Primary Source Findings

**Venue History:**
- The Warfield, 982 Market Street, San Francisco. Built as vaudeville theater, opened May 13, 1922 as Loews Warfield. Designed by architect G. Albert Lansburgh. Capacity 2,300.
- "Movie palace ornate" interior: marble, gilt, chandeliers, grand staircase to balcony. Lyrical mural above the proscenium arch painted by Albert Herter. Victorian influence, ornate ceiling designs, wrought-iron balustrades. Gold-leafed opera boxes overlook the palatial stage.
- First brought to Deadhead attention with the band's historic 15-date engagement in September-October 1980.
- Later became the home base of Jerry Garcia Band — Garcia played there around 100 times apart from the Dead.
- SOURCE: [Lost Live Dead](http://lostlivedead.blogspot.com/2016/10/the-fox-warfield-and-warfield-theater.html)

**The 1980 Acoustic/Electric Tour:**
- 15 shows at the Warfield (Sept 25 - Oct 14), then 2 at Saenger Theatre New Orleans, then 8 at Radio City Music Hall NYC.
- Three-set format: one acoustic set followed by two electric sets. First acoustic sets since early 1970s.
- 15th anniversary celebration / show of appreciation for loyal fans.

**Jerry Garcia Quote:**
- On the volume of material: "We ended with so much good material that it was a struggle. The idea of just one acoustic and one electric record was sort of pathetic, since our electric tunes are seldom less than eight minutes long. And that meant our fat electric album would have two songs on a side. It was kind of silly."

**Albums:**
- Reckoning (acoustic) and Dead Set (electric) — released 1981.
- The Warfield, San Francisco, CA 10/9/80 & 10/10/80 — released Record Store Day 2019.

## Upstate New York Venue & Touring Context
(From Lost Live Dead — comprehensive essay)

- **Why upstate NY:** Positioned between major markets (Boston, NYC, Philadelphia). Early reasoning: fill weeknights between profitable weekend bookings without extensive travel.
- **Promoters:** Harvey 'N' Corky Productions (Harvey Weinstein and Corky Burger) promoted Buffalo shows.
- **Mail-order tickets (1983):** Revolutionized regional attendance — fans could plan multi-city road trips.
- **Deadhead travel culture:** Late 1970s shift toward coordinated touring by fans, accelerated by high-quality tape circulation (especially Cornell '77 bootlegs).

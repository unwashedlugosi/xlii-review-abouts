## 1972-03-21 through 1972-03-28: Academy of Music, NYC (Seven Nights)

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Address:** 126 E. 14th Street, Manhattan
- **Capacity:** 3,387 seats (double balcony)
- **Architect:** Thomas W. Lamb (1927)
- **History:** Built as a William Fox deluxe movie palace. Converted to rock concert hall after Fillmore East closed in June 1971. Had a large Wurlitzer pipe organ (removed 1976) and prominent crystal chandelier. Later renamed The Palladium (1976). Demolished 1997 by NYU.
- **Source:** [Jerry's Brokendown Palaces](http://jerrygarciasbrokendownpalaces.blogspot.com/2011/09/academy-of-music-126-e-14th-street-new.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Patrick Carr, Grapevine (Toronto), May 17, 1972
*Possibly reprinted from Village Voice, April 6, 1972*
Title: "Why Is That Hairy Man Grinning?"
- Carr attended multiple nights. Highlighted Tuesday (3/28) as exceptional.
- **JERRY GARCIA QUOTE:** "It's really far out, just too fuckin' neat, man. I mean, we've only just started gettin' into what we can do."
- First and last nights identified as musical high points.
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: March 21-28](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2014/09/march-21-28-1972-academy-of-music-nyc.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Ernest Leogrande, Daily News, March 30, 1972
Title: "Winging Through the Night"
- Seven concerts in one week, all sold out within hours.
- Band composition: Garcia, Lesh, Weir, Kreutzmann, Pigpen, Keith Godchaux, Donna Godchaux.
- The week's concerts helped finance the two-month, seven-country European tour.
- Republished in Dick's Picks 30 booklet.
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: March 25](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2012/09/march-25-1972-academy-of-music-nyc.html)

## 1972-04-26: Jahrhunderthalle, Frankfurt

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Name:** Jahrhunderthalle (Century Hall)
- **Architect:** Friedrich Wilhelm Kraemer, opened 1963
- **Function:** Multipurpose concert/exhibition venue, designed for concert orchestras
- **Acoustics:** Bob Weir noted the hall was "made out of plastic" but produced "great-sounding" results. Phil Lesh was particularly enthusiastic about the space's design, which "affected the band's performance" (per co-manager John McIntire)
- **Source:** [Bozos & Bolos](http://bozosandbolos.blogspot.com/2012/04/42672-frankfurt-loser-stranger-barleys.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: German newspaper review, May 5, 1972 (reviewer: CSR)
- Called the concert "an experiment of total communication"
- Noted 50-member commune traveling together across the Atlantic
- Critic was largely negative on musical grounds (called it "simply bad")
- But acknowledged: "the hall was overflowing" with "thunderous applause everywhere"
- Noted most attendees were American, already familiar with commune living
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: April 26 Frankfurt](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2014/09/april-26-1972-jahrhunderthalle-frankfurt.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Jerry Garcia interview, Pop Magazine (Germany), August 1972
- Conducted after the Frankfurt concert
- **Garcia on cost:** "Approximately 10,000 DM per performance, but we bear all expenses ourselves."
- **Garcia on touring:** "We could never afford to drag 44 people across Europe. But since our record deal with Warner Brothers is about to expire...they have paid us a big advance."
- **Garcia on leadership:** "I do not consider myself a leader. I want to be seen as a musician, everything else does not interest me."
- **Garcia on concert structure:** "Usually we start a gig with material that is very familiar to us...we use the first 45 minutes of a performance to warm up ourselves"
- **Garcia on future plans:** "Something of our own, but nothing like Jefferson Airplane's 'Grunt.'"
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: April 1972 Garcia Interview](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2014/10/april-1972-jerry-garcia-interview.html)

### PERFORMANCE DETAILS
- Featured rare "The Stranger (Two Souls in Communion)" - Pigpen original, performed only three times on tour
- Released as Hundred Year Hall (1995) - first Dead album released after Garcia's death
- **Source:** [Bozos & Bolos](http://bozosandbolos.blogspot.com/2012/04/42672-frankfurt-loser-stranger-barleys.html)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **Garcia's Pop Magazine interview (10,000 DM, 44 people, Warner advance)** - primary source gold
- **German critic calling it "an experiment of total communication"** - unique perspective
- **Phil Lesh enthusiastic about the venue acoustics** (via McIntire)
- **Rare Pigpen original "The Stranger"** - only 3 performances
- **Friedrich Wilhelm Kraemer concert hall opened 1963**

## 1972-05-03: Olympia Theatre, Paris

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Name:** L'Olympia (now L'Olympia Bruno Coquatrix)
- **Address:** 28 Boulevard des Capucines, Paris 9th
- **Founded:** April 12, 1893 - first music hall in Paris
- **Founder:** Joseph Oller, who also co-founded the Moulin Rouge
- **Capacity:** ~2,000
- **History:** Originally featured acrobats, cross-dressers, and can-can dancers. Declined in 1920s, converted to cinema, reopened as venue in 1954 under Bruno Coquatrix. Hosted Edith Piaf, Beatles, James Brown, Bob Dylan. Saved from demolition in 1990s by preservation order.
- **Proximity:** Short walk from band's hotel at the Grand Hotel
- **Sources:** [Wikipedia: Olympia (Paris)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olympia_(Paris)), [Sortiraparis](https://www.sortiraparis.com/en/what-to-see-in-paris/concerts-music-festival/articles/287644-l-olympia-the-history-of-a-legendary-paris-venue)

### PERFORMANCE DETAILS (Bozos & Bolos)
- Energetic, vocal French crowd with "cat-calls and unison chants in both French and English" and "righteous eruptions of applause"
- Phil Lesh dominated the second half
- Band explored unusual jamming arrangements including instrumental duets
- First set highlight: Good Lovin' with Pigpen's passionate delivery
- **Source:** [Bozos & Bolos: Paris 5/3/72](http://bozosandbolos.blogspot.com/2012/05/5372-paris-hes-gone-may-day-maibock.html)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **First music hall in Paris, founded 1893 by Joseph Oller (Moulin Rouge co-founder)**
- **2,000-capacity venue where Piaf, Beatles, Brown had performed**
- **French crowd chanting in French and English** - unique cultural moment
- **Band staying at the Grand Hotel nearby**

## 1972-05-07: Bickershaw Festival, Wigan, UK

### VENUE/FESTIVAL DETAILS
- **Location:** Naylor's Farm, Bickershaw (population 1,200), near Wigan, Lancashire
- **Site:** 150-acre, adjacent to Bickershaw Colliery (coal mine)
- **Dates:** May 5-7, 1972 (Dead played Sunday)
- **Ticket Price:** £2.25
- **Attendance:** ~50,000 paying; ~20,000 additional free entry through fence breaches. 60,000 estimated for the Dead's set on Sunday.
- **Organizers:** Peter J. Harris and Harry Cohen (aka Bilkus), with Jeremy Beadle as artistic director
- **Stage:** Designed by architect Ian McCottrick, cost £9,000, featured video screens
- **Financial outcome:** Organizers lost £60,000 (~£120,000 budget vs ~£60,000 gate)
- **Sources:** [Write Wyatt UK](https://writewyattuk.com/2022/07/08/remembering-the-bickershaw-festival-50-years-on-back-in-conversation-with-chris-hewitt/), [UK Rock Festivals](https://www.ukrockfestivals.com/dead.html)

### WEATHER
- Rain throughout the weekend, creating knee-deep mud
- A high-dive act tank was emptied directly onto the area in front of the stage, creating a sea of mud
- "Cold but dry" Sunday morning, with clearing skies just before the Dead's set
- Bonfires built around the field's perimeter for warmth - "pagan festival" atmosphere

### PROFESSIONAL PRESS REVIEWS

**Nick Kent, Frendz Magazine, June 1972:**
- "The Dead played for five hours, maybe more...Fireworks exploded, freaks danced and the band went through every change conceivable."
- Called out "a beautiful 'Dark Star' and a sizzling Pigpen work out on 'Good Lovin''"
- On festival conditions: "Despite promises by the promoters of a flat, well drained site, too little sun and too much rain reduced the ground to one large mud pack."
- **ALSO ON ROCK'S BACKPAGES:** [Rock's Backpages: Nick Kent Bickershaw](https://www.rocksbackpages.com/Library/Article/grateful-dead-et-al-bickershaw-festival-lancashire)

**Melody Maker (Roy Hollingworth, Andrew Means, Chris Welch), May 13, 1972:**
Title: "The Day The Music Drowned"
- "For a full half hour or more, the Dead played up to their name. They were dead."
- "Changes began to happen around sunset...They were playing a succession of short sharp numbers."
- "The deep rumblings of Phil Lesh's bass chords and Bill Kreutzmann's drumming, the cutting guitar rhythms of Bob Weir, and most dramatically of all, Garcia's superb lead..."
- "The purple spotlights focused on Garcia, 'Dark Star' rebounded from atmospherics."
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: Bickershaw](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2014/09/may-5-7-1972-bickershaw-festival.html)

**Muther Grumble, June 1972:**
- "The site, near Wigan, can only be described as a mud bath...The Grateful Dead who played really excellent sounds for 5.5 hours that I can only describe as Far Out!"

**Nick Kent, Apathy for the Devil (2010 autobiography):**
- Described the event as "a sort of mud-caked psychedelic concentration camp...The Dead performed splendidly but there was no getting around the fact that the whole ugly debacle was destined to be acid rock's last hurrah."

### GARCIA QUOTES (pre-show interview)
- On playing despite rain: "Oh yeah, I think we're gonna play, yeah."
- On festival conditions: "Most of the people I think are, you know, sort of accepting what's going on."
- On barriers: "We don't need no barriers man, nobody's going to attack us."

### WEIR/LESH BANTER (from recordings)
- Weir on climbers: "You don't want to cause a blooming catastrophe, so get the fuck down."
- On fireworks: "In the future we want to aim those a little higher, whoever's doing that. Mighty pretty and mighty lethal."
- On St. Stephen request: "We done forgot St Stephen...We can't play it anymore, we don't know how."
- Lesh: "We got a slight technical problem here, which will no doubt be ironed out in mere seconds."
- Weir: "In the meantime, you can all huddle a bit closer together for warmth."
- On the smell: "I don't know what you're burning out there, but it smells rotten." / "Everybody's burning their old socks...and its disgraceful."

### SUPPORTING ACTS
Captain Beefheart, The Kinks, Donovan, Hawkwind, Wishbone Ash, Dr. John, New Riders of the Purple Sage, Brinsley Schwarz, Flamin' Groovies, Incredible String Band, Cheech & Chong

### CULTURAL IMPACT
- **Elvis Costello** (age 17): Stood in the mud amazed by the five-hour set. This performance convinced him to start a band.
- **Joe Strummer** (age 19): Camping in mud all weekend. Called Captain Beefheart's Saturday set "the best concert I ever went to in my life."
- Band celebrated Bill Kreutzmann's 26th birthday with "Happy Birthday" on stage.
- Coal miners' families entered the site in Sunday best when security departed.
- Locals sold hot tea from doorsteps.
- Jerry Garcia met fans backstage on Rolling Stones Mobile truck.
- Only show on the entire Europe 72 tour to feature both Dark Star AND The Other One.

### EYEWITNESS ACCOUNTS (UK Rock Festivals)
- "When the Dead came on later that day, I had doubts about them as a band...but live! they were in a class of their own."
- "People were calling out for certain songs...we looked at each other; it was a perfect moment."
- "It was constantly raining and wet - not that it mattered a bit: everyone was SO HAPPY to be there."
- "The people of that small mining community...restored my faith in humanity."
- Fireworks during Dark Star with crowd on the audience tape going "whooooo."
- New Riders preceded the Dead, "when the sky cleared into a glorious sunset. And then the Dead came on."

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **Melody Maker review: "The Day The Music Drowned"** — major British music press
- **Nick Kent in Frendz: "acid rock's last hurrah"** — professional journalism
- **Elvis Costello inspired to start a band** — verifiable cultural impact
- **Coal miners' families entering in Sunday best** — extraordinary cultural collision
- **Only Europe 72 show with both Dark Star and The Other One**
- **£2.25 ticket, Bickershaw Colliery adjacent, 60,000 for the Dead's set**
- **Weir's onstage banter** — hilarious and well-documented
- **5+ hours, fireworks during Dark Star, Happy Birthday for Kreutzmann**
- **Locals selling tea from doorsteps**

## 1972-07-18: Roosevelt Stadium, Jersey City

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Built:** 1937 at Droyer's Point, Jersey City
- **Baseball capacity:** 24,000; rock concert capacity ~20,000 (reserved seats including folding chairs in outfield)
- **History:** Originally hosted Jersey Giants (International League) 1937-1950. Hosted Jackie Robinson's professional debut in 1946. Largely unused after minor league baseball declined. Became available for summer rock bookings in 1972.
- **Promoter:** John Scher, as part of "Garden State Music Fair" summer series
- **Stage position:** Around second base
- **Source:** [Rock Prosopography 101](http://rockprosopography101.blogspot.com/2022/11/roosevelt-stadium-jersey-city-nj.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Robert Christgau, Newsday, July 30, 1972
- Hot and humid night
- Took Hudson Tubes (public transit) because of broken car muffler
- Band opened with "Promised Land" (Chuck Berry cover)
- Keith Godchaux causing "tuning delays until intermission"
- Christgau's companion Nadine Robinson said of Garcia: "You know, he really convinces you. He really makes it real."
- They left during second set. Got ride with a stockbroker who "he'd seen them better" and regretted Pigpen's absence.
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: Roosevelt Stadium](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2015/06/july-18-1972-roosevelt-stadium-jersey.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Asbury Park Press, July 20, 1972 (reviewer: Marty Packin)
- Noted "the immense parking lot filled with all kinds of hawkers" selling merchandise
- Enthusiastic crowd response
- **Attendance:** 19,000 fans (per Rock Prosopography 101)
- **Source:** [Rock Prosopography 101](http://rockprosopography101.blogspot.com/2022/11/roosevelt-stadium-jersey-city-nj.html)

### ADDITIONAL CONTEXT
- Tuesday night performance starting at 7:45 PM
- First major rock stadium concert format in the United States (part of Scher's new model)
- First NY-area show without Pigpen — fans didn't realize he was seriously ill
- Bob Dylan, accompanied by David Bromberg, was in the audience
- Fireworks display during the performance
- **Source:** Various (Dead Sources, Rock Prosopography 101)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **Jackie Robinson's professional debut venue (1946)** — powerful venue history
- **Christgau's companion: "He really convinces you. He really makes it real."** — Newsday
- **19,000 fans, reserved seating in an old baseball stadium**
- **Bob Dylan in the audience**
- **First NY-area show without Pigpen**
- **John Scher's "Garden State Music Fair"** — dawn of stadium rock

## 1972-08-12 through 1972-08-25: Berkeley Community Theater (Four Nights)

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Capacity:** 3,500 (with orchestra pit)
- **Configuration:** Reserved seating, numbered tickets (no dance floor)
- **Acoustics:** Superior; fixed seating appealed to promoter Bill Graham
- **Curfew:** Union house with strict midnight curfew penalties
- **Source:** [Cryptical Developments: August 1972](http://cryptdev.blogspot.com/2012/08/grateful-dead-august-1972.html)

### PRIMARY SOURCE: Ralph Gleason, Rolling Stone, September 28, 1972
- **All four nights (14,000 tickets) sold out by end of second day of ticket sales**
- **Ticket prices: $3.50-$5.50**
- **No opening acts** — Dead performed entire show each night
- Music began at 7:00 PM, went until 11:00 or 11:30 PM
- Half-hour intermission mid-evening
- "The music was superb" and audiences were "warm, friendly, appreciative and enthusiastic."
- Dead could "come down to a whisper and still keep it moving."
- Garcia: "one of the true original sounds"
- Keith Godchaux's "unusual pianistic sound"
- Donna Godchaux's "charming Southern-flavored voice"
- "All in all the week was pure joy."
- Positioned as returning to big-band-era concert practices (single headliner, extended performances)
- **Source:** [Dead Sources: Berkeley August 1972](http://deadsources.blogspot.com/2012/09/august-1972-berkeley-community-theater.html)

### ADDITIONAL DETAILS (Cryptical Developments - eyewitness)
**8/20 San Jose Civic Auditorium:**
- 3,300-capacity, mission-style WPA project (1936)
- Only Dead performance at this venue
- Keith's jazz piano provided "a powerful foil" to Garcia and Lesh's extended improvisations

**8/25 Berkeley (final night):**
- Unannounced opening set by New Riders of the Purple Sage (~1 hour)
- Phil Lesh performed "infrequent but always adventurous bass solo"
- Show concluded with "Sugar Magnolia" just before midnight curfew
- **Source:** [Cryptical Developments](http://cryptdev.blogspot.com/2012/08/grateful-dead-august-1972.html)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **Ralph Gleason in Rolling Stone: "All in all the week was pure joy"**
- **14,000 tickets sold out in two days, $3.50-$5.50**
- **No opening act, 4+ hours per night, 7 PM start**
- **Gleason: Dead could "come down to a whisper and still keep it moving"**
- **Return to big-band-era concert format** — Gleason's observation
- **Strict midnight curfew** — union house rules

## 1972-11-19: Hofheinz Pavilion, Houston (NOTE: reviews.json says "Hofstra University, Hempstead, NY" but this is actually Hofheinz Pavilion, Houston, TX)

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Hofheinz Pavilion**, University of Houston campus
- **Capacity:** 10,000 (original), named after Roy Hofheinz and his wife
- **Opened:** 1967
- **Design:** Seating bowl dug into the ground; enter at top
- **Also home to:** Houston Rockets (NBA, 1971-1975)
- **Source:** [Concert Archives](https://www.concertarchives.org/venues/hofheinz-pavilion)

### CONTEXT
- Two-night stand: November 18-19, 1972
- **Originally double-billed with The Allman Brothers Band** — Allmans cancelled due to Berry Oakley's death on November 11 (motorcycle accident, eerily similar to Duane Allman's death one year earlier)
- Band transitioning toward the jazzier 1973 sound, but "still a bunch of rough edges" linking to their more raucous past
- Dark Star on 11/19 was "feedback-and-Phil Lesh heavy"
- Second-ever performance of Weather Report Suite Prelude emerged from the Dark Star jam
- November 18 show released by Rhino Records (November 2014) for Record Store Day
- **Source:** [Daily Dose of the Dead](https://dailydoseofdead.wordpress.com/2015/11/19/today-in-grateful-dead-history-november-19-1972-hofheinz-pavilion-houston-tx/)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **Allman Brothers cancelled due to Berry Oakley's death 8 days earlier**
- **Second-ever Weather Report Suite Prelude emerged from Dark Star**
- **Transitional sound — jazzier 1973 emerging through 1972 edges**
- **10,000-seat arena on University of Houston campus, Rockets home court**

## 1973-02-09: Maples Pavilion, Stanford

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Roscoe Maples Pavilion**, Stanford University, Palo Alto
- **Capacity:** 7,500
- **Opened:** 1969, named after donor Roscoe Maples
- **Only the fourth concert ever held there** — evening shows banned from Frost Amphitheater in fall 1970
- **Sprung wooden floor** (designed for basketball) — towering speaker columns visibly swayed during performances
- **Source:** [Cryptical Developments: Feb 9 1973](https://cryptdev.blogspot.com/2023/02/the-dead-back-on-farm-february-9-1973.html)

### WALL OF SOUND PROTOTYPE DEBUT
- Prototype sound system that would evolve into the famous Wall of Sound
- Featured "stacks of hard truckers speakers and McIntosh amplifiers" behind the band
- **DAN HEALY QUOTE:** "We spent maybe $20,000 on amps, crossovers and stuff, started the show, and in the first two seconds of the song wiped out every brand-new tweeter."
- Folding chairs on the floor proved impractical; audiences dismantled and restacked them
- **Source:** [Cryptical Developments](https://cryptdev.blogspot.com/2023/02/the-dead-back-on-farm-february-9-1973.html)

### SEVEN SONG DEBUTS
Row Jimmy, Here Comes Sunshine, They Love Each Other, Eyes of the World, China Doll, Loose Lucy, Wave That Flag (later "U.S. Blues")

### PERFORMANCE DETAILS
- Winter 1973 tour opener
- Eyes of the World debut — considered one of the top five versions ever
- "They Love Each Other" described as "a full-blown celebration of a couple's palpable love for one another"
- **Source:** [Cryptical Developments](https://cryptdev.blogspot.com/2023/02/the-dead-back-on-farm-february-9-1973.html)

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **SEVEN song debuts in one night** — one of the most generative single shows in Dead history
- **Wall of Sound prototype: blew every tweeter in the first two seconds** (Dan Healy quote)
- **Only the fourth concert ever at the venue**
- **Sprung basketball floor made speaker stacks sway**
- **Eyes of the World debut — still considered top-5 version**
- **$20,000 in amps destroyed immediately**

## 1973-02-15: Dane County Coliseum, Madison, WI

### VENUE DETAILS
- **Dane County Coliseum** (now Dane County Expo Center)
- Floor covered the ice rink with a tarp
- One attendee stood "about 40 feet from the stage on the tarp covering the ice rink"
- **Weather:** Mean temperature 16°F; low dropped to -13°F within 36 hours

### HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE
- First of eight shows on the Dead's Midwest winter 1973 tour
- "The birth of a new era for the band"
- Second performances of several new songs debuted at Stanford 6 days earlier
- **DeadBase ranking:** #17 out of 250 in "Favorite Tapes" (DeadBase IX)
- Most-downloaded show on Internet Archive at one point

### PERFORMANCE HIGHLIGHTS
- Dark Star > Eyes of the World > China Doll — 45-minute sequence
- Only the second live performance of Eyes of the World
- Phil Lesh's extended bass solo with quadrophonic effect
- "Playin' In The Band" spans 15 minutes with "wonderful, spooky jam"
- Phil-Jerry transition into Eyes described as having "psalm-like" quality

### EYEWITNESS ACCOUNTS
- "As far as 2-15-73 is concerned, I still believe it is the epitome of the Grateful Dead"
- "A heat wave in the frozen tundra" / "the hall melted"
- One attendee: "very first Dead show" that "got me hooked"
- One attendee met their future spouse at the show but "caught strep"
- One got "sick as a dog" from cold
- **Dick Latvala:** "One of the top 10 shows of the year"
- **Source:** [Dead Essays: 2/15/73](http://deadessays.blogspot.com/2014/08/21573-dane-county-coliseum-madison.html)

### RECORDING NOTE
Original two-track reels missing from vault for years. Audio has tape clips and dropouts. Not a Betty Board recording. Source mysteriously vanished from Internet Archive in 2010.

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **-13°F outside, "the hall melted" inside**
- **Second-ever Eyes of the World**
- **Dark Star > Eyes > China Doll — 45 minutes of peak 1973**

## 1973-06-24: Portland Memorial Coliseum

## 1973-07-28: Watkins Glen Grand Prix Raceway, NY

### EVENT DETAILS
- **Official name:** Summer Jam at Watkins Glen
- **Venue:** 95-acre concert site at Watkins Glen Grand Prix racetrack
- **Attendance:** ~600,000 (Guinness World Record for largest audience at a pop festival)
- **Tickets sold:** ~150,000 at $10 each
- **Remaining 450,000:** Gained entry without tickets due to overwhelmed access points
- **Promoters:** Shelly Finkel and Jim Koplik
- **MC:** Bill Graham
- **Bands:** Grateful Dead, Allman Brothers Band, The Band
- **Source:** Multiple (uDiscover Music, NY State Troopers, nysmusic.com)

### CONTEMPORARY PRESS
**New York Times:**
- "At times the scene in the moist darkness resembled a Bosch painting — half-naked bodies coated with brown slime, moving rhythmically to the music amid huddled figures curled sleeping in the mud at their feet"

**Washington Post (Promoter Jim Koplik):**
- "We spent less than $30,000 on advertising...But we never dreamed we'd have this many people."

### PERFORMANCE DETAILS
- Dead played two extended sets opening the event, starting at noon with "Bertha"
- Featured "Eyes of the World" as a peak moment
- "Playing in the Band" closed the set
- Total Dead performance: nearly four hours
- **Soundcheck the previous day (July 27)** became legendary — turned into a two-set marathon featuring "Sugaree," "Tennessee Jed," "Wharf Rat." Released on So Many Roads (1965-1995) box set.

### EVENT DETAILS
- Weather interruption forced The Band to leave stage ~20 minutes during their set
- A skydiver fatality occurred during The Band's set
- Concert concluded with all-band jam: "Not Fade Away," "Mountain Jam," "Johnny B. Goode"
- Promoters originally connected after a successful Dead concert at Dillon Stadium, Hartford, CT in 1972

### STRONGEST FACTS FOR REVIEWS
- **600,000 people — Guinness World Record, bigger than Woodstock**
- **150,000 tickets at $10, 450,000 unpaid**
- **New York Times: "resembled a Bosch painting"**
- **Koplik: "we never dreamed we'd have this many people"**
- **Soundcheck so legendary it was officially released**
- **Bill Graham as MC**
- **Nearly four hours of Dead music**
